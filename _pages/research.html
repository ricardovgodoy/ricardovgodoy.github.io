Research

Manipulation, shared autonomy, and human–robot interfaces

Research on physical robots, with a focus on field and industrial tasks.

Mobile manipulation and navigation

Perception and control on physical robots

Work in this area covers object grounding, 3D reconstruction, grasp selection, collision-aware planning, and execution on Spot. Experiments include clutter, occlusion, incomplete depth, and calibration error.

Capability-aware traversability conditions predictions on the robot platform. Spot and a wheeled robot receive different maps from the same scene.

Language-guided mobile manipulationTarget grounding, partial geometry, grasping Open-vocabulary shared autonomyGuidance, collision avoidance, valve tasks Capability-aware navigationEmbodiment-conditioned traversability
Teleoperation and shared control

Human input in the control loop

The operator provides motion, target, or task commands. The robot handles collision avoidance, local planning, and final alignment.

Earlier work used EMG and lightmyography to decode gestures, dexterous motion, and grasping force. Current work also uses RGB-D body tracking, gaze, open-vocabulary target selection, and shared autonomy.
