Physical AI for real-world inspection and automation
I work on getting AI-driven robots from a lab capability to something that actually runs in the field. In practice that means owning the whole stack, from perception and task specification to control and evaluation, and making it survive latency, safety limits, partial observability, and calibration that is never quite right.
Dexterous manipulation in clutter
I work on grasping and manipulating unknown objects in clutter, combining vision with proprioceptive feedback and practical sensing. The aim is grasping that holds up in messy scenes, not just on tidy, well-separated objects.
Human-robot interaction for close-proximity tasks
I study interaction where people and robots share the same space, timing, and intent. That means designing behaviors and interfaces that stay predictable and safe when a task needs continuous adjustment rather than a pre-scripted motion.
Teleoperation and shared control
I design teleoperation where the operator supplies intent and the robot handles the rest: timing, collision avoidance, and local motion. The point is to get a task done reliably, not just to record a demonstration.
Wearable sensing and biosignal-driven intent (EMG and beyond)
I use wearable sensing, mostly EMG, to read a user's intent and turn it into robot control. This covers the signal processing, the intent decoding, and the control interface. A lot of the work is keeping it usable across everyday variation like electrode placement, fatigue, and motion artifacts.
Vision-Language Models for robot loco-manipulation
I look at how vision-language models can help a mobile robot act, grounding language and perception into manipulation in real scenes. That includes finding objects and affordances in clutter, guiding shared autonomy, and coping with partial views, latency, and conditions that change mid-task.
Assistive human-robot interaction
I develop assistive interaction that turns a person's intent into dependable help from a robot, through adaptive interfaces, shared autonomy, and real-time perception. Some of this is collaborative work on assistive quadrupeds for mobility support, where onboard sensing and intention-aware control provide help that fits the moment. I am also interested in how vision-language models can let these systems read a scene and adapt to the user.
Multimodal perception for manipulation
I combine several sensing streams (vision, motion capture when it is available, robot proprioception, and task context) to build manipulation pipelines that hold up under occlusion, clutter, and changing light.
Loco-manipulation on mobile platforms
I extend manipulation to mobile platforms like legged robots, where moving and manipulating have to be solved together. That calls for whole-body planning and control, and for interfaces that keep the system stable while it interacts with the world.
Robot self-learning in the real world
In the longer term, I want robots that keep improving from their own experience: collecting data as they act, noticing where they fail, and adapting to new settings with little human input. The priorities are safety, repeatability, and gains you can actually measure over time.
