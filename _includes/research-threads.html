Research

Manipulation, autonomy, and human input

Most projects are tested on physical systems under real-time, sensing, and collision constraints.

Research overview

Mobile manipulation and navigation

Open-vocabulary perception, grasping, collision-aware planning, and platform-conditioned navigation on physical robots.

  • Language-guided grasping and task grounding
  • Collision-aware shared and autonomous control
  • Mobile manipulation and embodiment-aware navigation

Teleoperation and human–robot interfaces

RGB-D, EMG, and lightmyography interfaces for motion, target, gesture, and force commands.

  • Teleoperation and shared autonomy
  • Wearable EMG and lightmyography interfaces
  • Assistive and close-proximity human–robot interaction
