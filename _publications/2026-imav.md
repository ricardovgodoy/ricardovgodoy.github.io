Vision-language models (VLMs) have recently enabled unprecedented generalization and flexibility for vision-language navigation (VLN) across multiple robotic platforms. On aerial robots, however, end-to-end VLN remains largely unexplored, particularly due to limited onboard compute, the complexity of hierarchical integration, and safety concerns. In this work, we present an aerial-native, fully onboard VLN framework with zero-shot generalization to unseen instructions and safety mechanisms designed for indoor flight. Specifically, we show that a quantized VLM paired with a fast B-spline-based 3D planner can support open-vocabulary object-goal navigation for aerial vehicles. For low-level control, we adopt a pretrained reinforcement learning policy that tracks planner-generated setpoints zero-shot across quadrotors. On top of this, we introduce lightweight safety measures that anticipate and mitigate the most common failure modes of VLN pipelines ported to a UAV stack. We deploy the full stack on a real UAV across 15 onboard flights in a controlled indoor volume, reaching a 13/15 success rate with 5.72 cm goal error and 32.91 cm tracking error, all computed onboard.
